22 Fun Facts About Dogs

Jokes
  1. A dog’s nose print is unique, much like a person’s fingerprint.

  2. Forty-five percent of U.S. dogs sleep in their owner’s beds.

  3. Speaking of sleeping … all dogs dream, but puppies and senior dogs dream more frequently than adult dogs.

  4. Seventy percent of people sign their dog’s name on their holiday cards.

  5. A dog’s sense of smell is legendary, but did you know that their nose has as many as 300 million receptors? In comparison, a human nose has about 5 million.

  6. Dogs’ noses can sense heat and thermal radiation, which explains why blind or deaf dogs can still hunt.

  7. The French Bulldog was first named the most popular breed in 2022.

  8. Yawning is contagious — even for dogs. Research shows that the sound of a human yawn can trigger one from your dog. And it’s four times as likely to happen when it’s the yawn of a person your pet knows.

  9. Dogs curl up in a ball when sleeping to protect their organs — a holdover from their days in the wild, when they were vulnerable to predator attacks.

  10. The Basenji is not technically “barkless,” as many people think. They can yodel.

  11. The Australian Shepherd is not actually from Australia. In fact, they are an American breed.

  12. The Labrador Retriever is originally from Newfoundland.

  13. Human blood pressure goes down when petting a dog. And so does the dog’s.

  14. There are over 75 million pet dogs in the U.S. — more than in any other country.

  15. Dogs are not color-blind. They can see blue and yellow.

  16. All puppies are born deaf.

  17. Dalmatians are born completely white. They develop their spots as they get older.

  18. Dogs have about 1,700 taste buds. We humans have between 2,000 and 10,000.

  19. When dogs kick backward after they go to the bathroom, it’s not to cover it up, but to mark their territory, using the scent glands in their feet.

  20. According to Guinness World Records, a Great Dane named Zeus is the world’s tallest male dog. Zeus is 3 feet, 5.18 inches tall.

  21. What about the shortest dog? According to Guinness World Records, the shortest dog ever recorded was Pearl the Chihuahua. She measures 3.59 inches tall.
